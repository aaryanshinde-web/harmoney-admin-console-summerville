# Manage Content — Terms & Conditions

> Stage a new Terms and Conditions version, schedule it against a regulatory effective date, and inspect the published record that an examiner expects to see.

## Summary

Terms & Conditions is where UDAAP, Reg-E, and other disclosures are staged, versioned, and made Forced or Optional at the module level. The register opens to the full list of published agreements with Module / Status filters and an explicit Pacific Standard Time disclosure above the table, so no reviewer ever has to guess which clock a window refers to.

New Terms and Conditions captures a full governance payload — Title, Version No, Channels Type, Module Type, Scope, OnScreen Notification Message, Terms Type, and an uploaded file. Schedule toggles between Immediate and Later; Later exposes a Select Date picker, which is how Compliance stages a disclosure ahead of its effective date and lets the platform flip the version on automatically at midnight PST.

## Key Use Cases

Compliance has a new Reg-E disclosure that must take effect on a specific date. The operator opens Terms and Conditions, uploads the revised file with Terms Type Forced, Scope Existing Users & New Enrollments, and channel ALL, sets Schedule to Later with the regulatory effective date, and the platform flips it on automatically without anyone needing to be on console that morning.

An examiner asks which version of the Wire Transfer agreement was live on a specific date. The operator opens the Terms and Conditions register, clicks the Title, and the View Terms page shows Title, Version No, Scope, Terms Type, Schedule Status, and Effective Terms Date — the complete auditable record.

## End-to-End Workflow

### Prerequisites

- Manage Content role assigned to the operator via Manage Administrators (separate roles for Marketing, Compliance, and Content Operations where the bank has chosen to split duties).
- Approved marketing creative (1000x1296 banner image), approved legal document (PDF for Terms and Conditions), or approved broadcast copy for Notifications, reviewed and signed off outside the console.
- For scheduled content: a confirmed effective date or send timestamp in Pacific Standard Time, aligned with the regulatory, campaign, or operations calendar.
- For audience targeting: content-segmentation Groups already provisioned via Add Group so the operator can scope a banner or notification to the intended audience.

### Step-by-Step Flow

#### Step 1 — Open Terms and Conditions to review the published register

From Manage Content, click Terms and Conditions to land on the versioned register of published legal agreements. The default Module / Status filters and the timezone disclosure (All dates are in Pacific Standard Time) are the controls you need for any compliance review, and the Add new terms and conditions button is the entry point for every new version.

![Step 1 — Open Terms and Conditions to review the published register](img/mc-07-terms-and-conditions-list.jpg)

#### Step 2 — Open New Terms and Conditions and capture the full governance payload

Click Add new terms and conditions to open the New Terms and Conditions form. Capture Title, Version No, Channels Type (ALL / Mobile / Online), Module Type, Scope (Existing Users & New Enrollments or New Enrollments only), OnScreen Notification Message, Terms Type (Forced / Optional), and upload the file — every field on this form is a governance contract, so the defaults should reflect Compliance's published matrix for that module.

![Step 2 — Open New Terms and Conditions and capture the full governance payload](img/mc-08-terms-add-new.jpg)

#### Step 3 — Toggle Schedule to Later to expose the Select Date picker

On the New Terms and Conditions form, toggle Schedule from Immediate to Later — the form reveals a Select Date field. This is how a Reg-E or UDAAP disclosure is staged ahead of its effective date: Compliance uploads the file now, sets the effective date here, and the platform flips the version on automatically at midnight PST on that date, without anyone needing to be on console.

![Step 3 — Toggle Schedule to Later to expose the Select Date picker](img/mc-09-terms-schedule-later.jpg)

#### Step 4 — Open a Terms row to see the published record on the View Terms page

From the Terms and Conditions register, click a Title to open the View Terms detail page. The page shows the complete governance payload — Title, Version No, Module Type, Scope, OnScreen Notification Message, Terms Type, Schedule Status, Effective Terms Date, Status — and a View Content button to surface the agreement body, which together form the auditable record an examiner will want to see.

![Step 4 — Open a Terms row to see the published record on the View Terms page](img/mc-10-terms-view-detail.jpg)
